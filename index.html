Professional Teaching Journey & Educational Philosophy

My journey in education began at age 15, immediately after completing junior high school, when I started working in early childhood education. In that role, I focused on developing children's foundational numeracy and literacy skills while incorporating creative activities such as poetry recitation and delivering the full early childhood curriculum. This formative experience ignited my lifelong passion for teaching and profoundly shaped the core principles of my educational philosophy.

After six months, I enrolled in Senior High School as a Science student. Upon graduation, I returned to assist my former Junior High School teacher at her school, where I worked as a teaching assistant. In that role, I supported her in delivering Science, Citizenship Education, and Religious and Moral Education. Teaching across different subjects and age groups provided me with broad and invaluable classroom experience.

Key Transition: From Classroom to Innovation

My formal career as a classroom teacher officially began when I was appointed to teach Stage 6 pupils. After seven rewarding months in that role, I gained admission to Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, where I pursued and successfully completed a Bachelor's degree in Biochemistry.

During my undergraduate studies, I completed clinical biochemistry internships where I conducted patient laboratory tests and further solidified my scientific knowledge. Although I had initially envisioned a career as a medical laboratory scientist, stringent licensing requirements ultimately guided me back to education. This transition reaffirmed teaching as my true calling and life's work.

I completed my national service as a Junior High School Science teacher, an experience that further deepened my commitment to the teaching profession. Immediately afterward, I enrolled in a Master's in Education (M.Ed.) program. Throughout the course, I discovered that my teaching style was distinctly innovative and frequently diverged from traditional approaches.

Pedagogical Transformation

A pivotal moment came when I received specialized training in modern pedagogies, particularly the Flipped Classroom approach. Combined with my natural teaching strengths, this training transformed me into a specialized STEM educator capable of delivering IGCSE curricula in Biology, Physics, and Chemistry, including A-Level courses.

One of the highlights of my career was teaching IGCSE A-Level Biology, where student feedback was overwhelmingly positive. Learners consistently praised my innovative methods and the profound impact these approaches had on their understanding and academic growth.

Balancing full-time teaching with graduate studies was demanding, yet I completed my M.Ed. without interrupting my classroom responsibilities. In my final two years of teaching, I fully embraced Inquiry-Based strategies and led a school-endorsed four-week Professional Development program on Project-Based Learning for colleagues.

Student-Athlete Success Stories

My work with student-athletes proved especially rewarding. My graduate thesis, titled "The Effects of Inquiry-Based Learning on Student-Athletes' Classroom Engagement and Academic Performance," produced outstanding results. The approach dramatically improved academic outcomes for Pre-IGCSE athletes and successfully prepared IGCSE athletes transitioning to American high schools on sports scholarships.

Throughout my career, student feedback and formative assessment data became essential tools for continuously refining my practice. Recognizing the critical role of technology for 21st-century learners, I integrated EdTech into my classroom, an experience that ultimately led me to found Stellar-Scholar, a comprehensive multi-tenant educational technology platform designed to transform how students interact with content and assessments.

Although I continue to teach actively, I have expanded my work into educational consulting and EdTech entrepreneurship. Building on my earlier experience as a consultant for English Hubs teachers, where I supported colleagues in adopting effective teaching methodologies, I now bring the same depth of classroom insight to a broader stage. My journey from early childhood education to delivering advanced IGCSE and A-Level STEM curricula, grounded in research-backed innovative approaches and technology integration, uniquely positions me to partner with schools and educators to strengthen teaching practices and drive better student outcomes.

Project-Based Learning Framework

My Project-Based Learning (PBL) framework engages students deeply with real-world content by starting with a thought-provoking driving question and an immersive launch, such as a field trip, video, or real-life problem statement. Students then conduct sustained inquiry, choose how to showcase their learning through presentations, prototypes, posters, models, or other formats, and follow a clear plan that incorporates regular peer and teacher feedback with iterative refinement to achieve high-quality outcomes. The project concludes with a public presentation to an authentic audience during school assemblies, exhibition weeks, or similar events, followed by meaningful written and verbal reflection. This process cultivates lasting understanding, critical thinking, collaboration, creativity, and student ownership while seamlessly developing essential 21st-century skills and confidence in addressing complex challenges.

Quick Tip: Student choice in project topics boosts motivation and ownership.

Science Education Innovation

My science teaching methodology is built around making complex concepts not just accessible, but genuinely exciting and memorable for students. I achieve this by deliberately blending rigorous content with elements from the liberal arts, motion graphics, cartoons, and meaningful storytelling that students already love. I frequently draw on powerful visual and narrative references from popular culture: scenes from X-Men illuminate mutation and genetic variation, episodes of Primal bring competition and natural selection to life in ecosystems, and carefully selected movie clips or animated shorts serve as vivid hooks that instantly connect abstract ideas to emotions and experiences students recognize. These references act as cognitive bridges, transforming potentially dry topics into stories that feel relevant and human. Hands-on engagement remains at the heart of every lesson. Simple, dramatic demonstrations (such as the classic magnesium + hydrochloric acid reaction with the "squeaky pop" hydrogen test) consistently produce audible gasps and wide-eyed excitement, while PhET simulations allow students to manipulate variables in ways no textbook can match. These experiences turn passive learning into active discovery. I place particular emphasis on student-led exploration: lessons often begin with their own "what if" questions, which we pursue together through guided web research, interactive simulations, and structured discussions designed to surface and resolve misconceptions in real time. This inquiry-driven approach, combined with storytelling, visuals, and tangible experiments, ensures that even the most challenging topics in Biology, Chemistry, and Physics are understood deeply, remembered longer, and, most importantly, genuinely enjoyed.

Quick Tip: Incorporate technology like PhET simulations for interactive learning.
